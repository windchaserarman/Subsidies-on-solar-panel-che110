What is Solar Subsidy?

A solar subsidy is like a financial bonus or help that you can get when you decide to use solar energy for your home or business. It's a way for the government or other organizations to encourage people to use solar power by making it a bit cheaper or more financially rewarding.



Why does Government give Solar Subsidy?

India aimed to install 40GW of residential solar panels but reached only 5GW by 2021. The revised target is 280GW by 2030, necessitating an annual installation of 10GW. The government encourages home installations, allowing customers to choose any solar provider. After installation, photos are sent to the local electricity board.
Governments offer solar subsidies to promote:

  1. Clean Energy Adoption
  2. Emission Reduction
  3. Energy Independence
  4. Job Creation
  5. Technological Innovation
  6. Grid Reliability
  7. Renewable Targets
  8. Consumer Empowerment
  9. Public Awareness
  10. Long-Term Economic and Environmental Benefits

Subsidy Benefits

There are three parties involved in the process: 1. Customers, 2. Channel Partners, 3. DISCOMS.

#1. Customer Benefits

The subsidy is only for homesâ€”individual ones and big apartments, not for businesses. It's only for solar systems connected to the grid, without a battery. Since getting a solar system is a big expense, the subsidy helps people by giving them financial support. It makes it easier for them to invest in solar energy and lightens the financial load..

Homeowners can only install a solar system and claim subsidy through the state DISCOM. They will share customer details and register the nearest channel partner. You can find all states DISCOMS from here.

#2. Channel Partner Benefits

Channel Partners gain more customers and business. While a regular person may find it challenging to navigate the subsidy application process, even if they really want it, that's where the Channel Partner steps in. They collaborate with the customer and government departments to speed up the subsidy application process, making it easier for the customer.

#3. DISCOMS Benefits

Homes use less power, so they can contribute more electricity to businesses. We understand that businesses need more electricity than homes.
The customer is happy with the financial help, the Channel Partner is happy with a new customer, and the government is pleased because it's closer to reaching its solar installation goal.

#2. Solar Panel Subsidy in West Bengal

West Bengal is a pioneer in solar technology in India. Government-owned WEBEL was one of the first manufacturers of solar energy panels in the country. It is mandatory for all large housing societies in West Bengal, (having a total contract demand of more than 500 kW) to install solar rooftop systems to meet a minimum of 1.5% of their total electrical load by solar energy. The subsidies are applicable as per SECI guidelines.
